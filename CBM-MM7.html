CLASS BASED MODIFICATION GUIDE

The Mega Man 7 category covers the characters from the original Mega Man 7/Rockman 7 game and it's remakes/spin-offs.


FREEZE
MAN
Description Role Health Speed Jump
Terrain Control indirect DPS class, focused on ground control. 100 0.8/0.78 10
Main Fire Alt-Fire Item Extra
Projectile that splits on walls. If it hits the floor it leaves an icicle that cause contact damage. If it hits the ceiling, it drops an icicle that shatters on the floor. A super charged shot that will send waves through the floor. If you hit the floor or a wall, if will send multiple waves that will create icicles and freeze foes on place. If you hit ceiling, it will cause a huge amount of icicles to drop. None None
Info Tips
Freeze is a class with a lot of ground utilities. The icicles and shards help cover wide areas of the ground, and both the ground and ceiling versions of the alt-fire are capable of denying entire rooms. Breaking into team fights and droping your alt-fire will instantly disperse your enemies. Unless you are directly trading 1 on 1, you will always be better aiming your shots at close surfaces, since you can get way more use from the icicles and shards than direct shots.

JUNKMAN
Description Role Health Speed Jump
Terrain Control protector class, focused on passage denial. 125 0.6/0.58 14
Main Fire Alt-Fire Item Extra
Creates a solid block of junk. Can be punched forward using Alt-fire. Melee punch that will throw out small crumplets of junk. Summons a shield that reduces damage and causes contact damage. Refreshing the shield will cause you to release metal scraps around you. Can pick up scrap metal.
Info Tips
Junk's game will be trying to get as much scraps as possible to get a full scrap bar and activite the full shield, from that point forward, the most effective strategy is to close in enemies and harass them with the junk blocks, either causing heavy damage by summoning it over them/punching it on them or to close passages using the blocks. Even if you don't have a lot of scraps, you can still trade safely by activating junk shield and hugging someone and punching them for both contact and melee damage respectively if you are in a tight spot.

BURSTMAN
Description Role Health Speed Jump
Demolition indirect DPS class, focused on mine laying. 100 0.8/0.78 10
Main Fire Alt-Fire Item Extra
Releases a bubble carrying a bomb. It will explode on contact with enemies or drop it if it hits a wall or ceiling. Will plant a mine if it hits the floor though. Will carry extra mines if your Mine bar has charges left. Pinpoint attack that will releases bubbles. Will trap foes preventing them from moving or can be used to give yourself limited flight. Mine Charge: Will charge your Mine bar.

Mine Release: If you are using your Bubble Ride, you will drops a few mines on the floor if your Mine bar has charges left.		 None
Info Tips
Burst's danger wrap mines are the key feature of this class. Their fuse is not that long, but they more than make up for that by being able to be dropped from a distance, allowing you to pierce some defences by hitting walls or ceiling. Save your Bubble Cages for either sniping or flying around. If you ever need an extra boost to the reach of your Bubble Cage, you can spawn it close by and try to jump on it on the end of it's tragectory, allowing you to start with extra height.

CLOUDMAN
Description Role Health Speed Jump
AOE off-tank class, focused on spliting attacks. 125 0.6/0.58 10
Main Fire Alt-Fire Item Extra
Shots that split on contact. Will not split if you are out of ammo though. When on the ground, will recharge half of your ammo bar. While on on flight mode, will send thunderbolts to the ground. Toggles flight On/Off. Has reduced gravity.
Info Tips
With Cloud, there is really no good reason to fight without ammo, so pay attention to your supply and never overextend your flights. Recharge every time it is possible and use your flights more for crowd control than direct combat. Remember that the vertical thunder bolts can crawl up walls. Just because you need to activate flight, it doesn't mean that you always need to be above your enemies to hit them. Remember that and take them by surprise.

SPRING
MAN
Description Role Health Speed Jump
Mobility direct DPS class, focused on mixing jump arcs. 100 0.76/0.74 20
Main Fire Alt-Fire Item Extra
Throws springs that bounce once before spliting into 2. Tapping will do a quick punch that explodes. Holding will make you stand still and do a stronger punch. None None
Info Tips
Spring's extremely high mobility is extremely useful. Being already hard to hit thanks to the their jump, you can easily throw enemies off by surprising your enemies with a sudden air brake thanks to your held alt-fire. Keeping it mixed up is important with this class. Before spliting, the Wild Coil causes way more damage than the each split shot. If you are 1 on 1, the direct shot is way more effective.

SLASHMAN
Description Role Health Speed Jump
Melee direct DPS class, focused on high speed shanking. 85 0.82/0.8 10
Main Fire Alt-Fire Item Extra
Melee swipe that gets stronger with more dashes. Dashes forward leaving goop balls that ensnare enemies. Dashing into a wall resets velocity allowing you to leap on surfaces. None Can wall jump up to 5 times before landing.
Info Tips
With Slash, you will practically be spending most of your time alternating in between waiting your bar to charge or charging into your targets. Learn how to use your wall dashes to make impressive U-Turns or triangle jumps and get your foes by surprise with a fully charged Slash Claw. The goop that you leave on your dash is still useful. It can slow enemies down and make it easier to hit them even if you miss your charged attack. Also, they stay on the ground, so you can make the ground into a minefield.

SHADEMAN
Description Role Health Speed Jump
Vampire indirect DPS class, focused on surprise stikes. 100 0.8/0.78 10
Main Fire Alt-Fire Item Extra
A ricocheting shot. Will be charged if you intercet your own ricocheting shots. Flies upward. Main Fire while flying causes you to swoop down, grabing an enemy and stunning them. While they are stunned, you will steal their health. Pressing Alt-fire cancels it by throwing them away. Nope If you use your swoop to instead grab an ally, you will instead carry them around allowing them attack while you fly with them.
Info Tips
With Shade, you can take every oportunity possible to get a charged Noise Crusher, since it will cause a damage close to 3 normal shots. Save the flight and grab for either finishing or escaping, since you can take one enemy hostage and use the armor you get while in that state to survive on your escape. Also, if you can, give your team a lift, it will prove very useful on the long run. You can actually attack right after you release your enemy from you grab. Use that opportunity to get 1 or 2 clean hits without any resistance.

TURBOMAN
Description Role Health Speed Jump
Rushing direct DPS class, focused on ramming into enemies. 100 0.8/0.78 10
Main Fire Alt-Fire Item Extra
Charges a flaming wheel that increases your movement speed. If it is fully charged it will stop giving a movement buff but instead will leave a fire trail when released. A ramming dash that can be charged to increase duration. Leaves a trail of fire. None None
Info Tips
Turbo is an amazing class to get into a fight if you can get either of your attacks charged beforehand. The charged Scorch Wheel will leave a dense trail of fire that can be used to deny spaces like corridors, and a fully charged ramming attack can leave a very spread and long trail of fire around to cut out entire rooms of access for the enemies. After entering the fight, just start trading normally. Don't try to use the charged version of your ramming attack on duels, as you will be defenseless during this charges, just the tapping of the alt-fire is enough to leave a small trail and ran into your foe before comming back trading with uncharged Scorch Wheels.
